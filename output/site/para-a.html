Broader view — Indian IT job market

The structural split

India's IT sector is not collapsing it is bifurcating. The traditional IT services model, which absorbed hundreds of thousands of freshers for routine coding and testing work, is contracting. Meanwhile, Global Capability Centres (GCCs) of multinationals are building deep engineering teams in Bengaluru, Hyderabad, and Pune paying 30% more than legacy IT firms.

Fresher pipeline Updated 2026-06-07

Fresher hiring among tier-1 IT firms remains sharply below FY22 peaks, with many from the 2023 batch still facing deferred joining dates. This reflects a structural shift by IT majors towards calibrated, skill-based fresher intake rather than mass recruitment. Proactive skill development in in-demand areas and targeted job searches are more crucial than ever given these persistent market conditions.

Where opportunities actually are Updated 2026-07-01

GCCs remain a robust growth area, now significantly bolstered by AWS's $1 billion commitment to embedded AI engineering units in India. Fintech is seeing strong tailwinds, with Cashfree expanding cross-border payments and a consortium including Visa and Mastercard launching a new global stablecoin. Demand for mid-career developers (3-7 years experience) persists, though competition is heightened by global tech restructuring.

Skills that change your odds Updated 2026-07-01

AI fluency, covering GitHub Copilot, LLM API integration, and RAG pipelines, remains paramount, further emphasized by AWS's $1 billion investment in embedded AI engineering units. The 'rolling bubbles' nature of the AI boom highlights continuous, adaptable upskilling for premium salaries. Cloud, DevOps, and data engineering roles grow at 7% YoY, while emerging demand for regulatory and compliance tech skills, particularly in agentic AI governance, makes tailored, keyword-optimised resumes mandatory for an 80% ATS threshold.
